Privacy Policy

Effective April 16, 2026 · Last updated April 16, 2026

1. Introduction

Stow (“we,” “our,” or “the app”) is an iOS envelope budgeting application developed by Koumo. We built Stow on a foundational principle: your financial data belongs to you and stays on your device.

This Privacy Policy explains what information Stow collects (very little), how it is used, and your rights regarding that information.

2. What Stow Does Not Collect

We want to start with what we do not do, because this list is longer — and more important — than the list of things we collect.

  • No bank account linking. Stow never connects to your bank, credit card provider, or any financial institution. All transactions are entered manually by you.
  • No user accounts. There is no sign-up, no email address, no password, no social login. You open the app and start budgeting.
  • No third-party analytics or tracking SDKs. We do not embed Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude, or any similar service.
  • No advertising. There are no ads in Stow, and no advertising identifiers are collected or shared.
  • No App Tracking Transparency (ATT) prompt. Because there is nothing to track, Stow never requests permission to track you across other apps or websites.
  • No server infrastructure for user data. We do not operate servers that receive, store, or process your budget data.

3. Data Stored on Your Device

All of your budgeting data — envelopes, transactions, budgets, period history, and settings — is stored locally on your iPhone using Apple’s SwiftData framework. This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly choose to export it (see Section 5) or enable iCloud Sync (see Section 4).

If you delete the app, your local data is permanently removed from the device.

4. iCloud Sync (Optional, Premium Feature)

If you subscribe to Stow Premium and enable iCloud Sync in Settings, your budget data is synced across your Apple devices using Apple’s CloudKit framework.

  • Your data is stored in your personal iCloud account, in a CloudKit private database that only you can access.
  • We (the developer) cannot read, access, or retrieve your synced data. Apple’s CloudKit private database architecture does not grant developers access to user data.
  • Sync is off by default. You must explicitly enable it.
  • You can disable sync at any time. To remove iCloud data, use iCloud storage management in iOS Settings.

iCloud Sync is governed by Apple’s privacy policy.

5. Data Export

Stow Premium users can export their data as CSV or PDF files. Export is entirely user-initiated via the iOS share sheet. Exported files are sent wherever you choose (AirDrop, email, Files, etc.). We do not receive a copy of exported data.

6. Subscription Management & RevenueCat

Stow offers optional premium subscriptions (monthly, annual, and lifetime) managed through Apple’s App Store. To handle subscription validation and entitlements, we use a third-party service called RevenueCat.

6.1 What RevenueCat receives

  • An anonymous, randomly generated user identifier (not linked to your name, email, or Apple ID).
  • Purchase and subscription events (e.g., started, renewed, cancelled, expired).
  • Basic device metadata (device type, OS version, app version) for subscription diagnostics.
  • Subscription status (active, expired, trial, etc.).

6.2 What RevenueCat does not receive

  • Any budget data whatsoever — no envelopes, transactions, amounts, categories, or financial information of any kind.
  • Your name, email address, or Apple ID.
  • Your location, contacts, photos, or any on-device data.

RevenueCat’s privacy practices are governed by their privacy policy.

7. App Store Connect Analytics

We have access to aggregated, anonymised analytics provided by Apple through App Store Connect (downloads, sessions, retention, crash reports). This data is collected and anonymised by Apple and cannot identify individual users. It is governed by Apple’s privacy policy.

8. Widgets

Stow offers home screen widgets. Widget data is stored locally in a shared App Group container on your device. It is not transmitted to any server.

9. Children’s Privacy

Stow does not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 (or the applicable age in your jurisdiction). Since Stow does not collect personal information from any user, this risk is inherently minimal. If you believe a child has provided personal information through the app, please contact us.

10. Data Retention

Your budget data is stored on your device (and optionally in your iCloud account), so you have full control over its retention. Deleting the app removes all local data. To remove iCloud data, manage your iCloud storage through iOS Settings.

RevenueCat retains anonymous subscription records in accordance with their own retention policy.

11. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction (GDPR, CCPA, Australian Privacy Act, and similar frameworks), you may have the right to access, correct, delete, or port your personal data.

  • Access & portability: Use the CSV or PDF export feature (Premium) to obtain a copy of all your data.
  • Deletion: Delete the app to remove all local data. Manage iCloud data through iOS Settings.
  • Correction: Edit any transaction or envelope directly in the app.

For rights related to RevenueCat’s processing of anonymous subscription data, contact us and we will coordinate on your behalf.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will update the “Last Updated” date at the top and, where practical, notify you through the app.
